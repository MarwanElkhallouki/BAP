\begin{abstract}
In the context of a significant increased use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) in sectors such as predictive maintenance and computer vision (CV), this paper focuses on the challenge of detecting and handling model degradation using Explainable AI (XAI) and Drift-Detection algorithms (DD).
XAI techniques such as LIME \autocite{garreau2020explaining} and SHAP \autocite{VandenBroeck2022} give a broader image of models experiencing DD.
Combining these techniques with DD algorithms such as MDDM \autocite{Pesaranghader2017} and EDDM \autocite{baena2006early} significantly increases the detection and interpretability of model-degradation.
Using the CMAPSS (NASA) \autocite{Saxena2008} as a case for time-based degradation in predictive maintenance, this paper proves this hypothesis.
The ImageNet-C \autocite{Hendrycks2019} is used to evaluate if the same model pipeline and techniques can used to evaluate real world examples of model degradation due to faulty sensors or cameras, which contributes to improving trust in AI-systems used in critical fields.
The results of this paper aims for an increased understanding in model-degradation and drift, and the ways in which to negate these factors in real world application.
\end{abstract}
